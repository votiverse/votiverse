Governance configuration

Every knob a group can turn — and which ones fit which scale.

A Votiverse group governs itself through a 10‑parameter configuration plus a set of toggleable capabilities. The parameters are deliberately few and fully orthogonal: every combination is valid, so the space doubles as an experiment surface — vary one axis, hold the rest, observe what changes.

Governance rules live in GovernanceConfig (delegation · ballot · timeline). Capabilities (voting, scoring, surveys, community notes) are separate, per‑group toggles owned by the backend. Predictions are a platform feature, always on. Six named presets are curated points in the space; any of them can be customized.

The parameter space

10 parameters, 3 groups

Each parameter is a governance decision a group creator can reason about — not an implementation dial. Defaults shown are from the recommended default preset, LIQUID_DELEGATION.

Delegation

2 · booleans
candidacyboolean · def true Formal candidate declaration. When on, members can declare as candidates, publish profiles, and appear in delegate discovery. In proxy mode (transferable off), only declared candidates can receive delegations.
transferableboolean · def true Transitive delegation. When on, voting power flows through chains (A→B→C means C carries A's weight). When off with candidacy on, delegation is a single hop — classic proxy.
Derived, not configured: delegation exists when candidacy || transferable · topic‑ and issue‑scoped delegation are always available · chain depth is unlimited when transferable, 1 hop in proxy mode · delegations are always revocable.

Ballot

5 · how votes are counted & seen
secretboolean · def true Are individual votes hidden from other members?
liveResultsboolean · def false Are aggregate tallies visible while voting is still open? (Sealed results prevent bandwagon effects.)
allowVoteChangeboolean · def true Can participants change their vote during the voting period?
quorumnumber 0–1 · def 0.1 Minimum fraction of members who must vote for the result to be valid. 0 = no quorum.
methodenum · def "majority" majority or supermajority. The supermajority threshold defaults to 2/3.
Derived, not configured: delegates are always accountable to their delegators — a delegator can see how their delegate voted regardless of ballot secrecy · participation is always voluntary.

Timeline

3 · phase durations, in days
deliberationDaysint ≥ 1 · def 7 Proposals are submitted, endorsed, and community‑noted. Issues can be added or cancelled in this phase.
curationDaysint ≥ 0 · def 2 Admins curate the voting booklet and write recommendations. 0 = no curation phase; the system auto‑selects the highest‑endorsed proposals.
votingDaysint ≥ 1 · def 7 The voting window. Votes outside it are rejected by the engine.
Lifecycle: deliberation → curation → voting → closed. The issue set (the ballot) is editable until voting opens; nothing during voting; the result is a permanent record after close.

Why each exists

Preset rationale

01Liquid Delegation default

Liquid delegation plus candidate accountability. Secret ballot shields voters from pressure; sealed results prevent bandwagons. The synthesis described in Paper I.

Fits: any group; a safe, well‑rounded start.

Direct Democracy

Everyone votes on everything. No delegation, no curation — zero governance overhead for groups small enough not to need it.

Fits: small clubs, parent committees, informal collectives.

Swiss Votation

Direct democracy with structured deliberation. A curation phase produces a voting booklet; a 20% quorum guards legitimacy. Inspired by the Swiss popular vote.

Fits: cooperatives, associations, civic groups.

Liquid Open

Informal liquid democracy for high‑trust groups where everyone knows each other. Public ballots, live results, short timelines, no formal candidates.

Fits: tech communities, professional bodies, mid‑size orgs.

Representative

Classic proxy voting: appoint a declared candidate who votes for you but can't pass it further. 50% quorum and short timelines for focused decisions.

Fits: boards, HOAs, unions, formal committees.

Civic Participatory

Liquid delegation at municipal scale. Two‑week deliberation and voting windows accommodate broad, diverse participation.

Fits: cities, participatory budgeting, citizen assemblies.

Beyond GovernanceConfig

Capabilities — independently toggled layers

Governance rules define how a vote works. Capabilities decide which activities a group runs at all. They live in the backend's per‑group capability registry (group_capabilities), not in GovernanceConfig, and each is switched on or off independently.

voting

Voting

Proposals, delegation, ballots, tallies — the full decision engine governed by the 10 parameters above.

scoring

Scoring

Rubric‑based, multi‑criteria ranking of options or candidates (the @votiverse/scoring package). Its own draft→open→closed lifecycle.

surveys

Surveys

Sentiment surveys decoupled from binding votes. Always anonymous, and non‑delegable by hard architectural rule.

community_notes

Community notes

Crowd‑sourced context on proposals and candidacies, evaluated by the community.

Predictions are deliberately not a capability — they are a platform‑level feature, always available wherever the UI offers the affordance. roles: owner · admin · member (backend‑owned, per group)

For research

The space as an experiment surface

The point of keeping the space small and fully orthogonal is that every combination is valid — there are no illegal states to design around. Hold every parameter fixed, vary one, and you have a clean A/B on a governance mechanism.

5 booleans × 1 two‑value method enum = 64 discrete points, multiplied by the continuous quorum and the three timeline ranges — small enough to enumerate, expressive enough for real experiments.

“Which system produces better outcomes at this scale, for this kind of group?”
